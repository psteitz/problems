\section*{Problem}
5,915,587,277 is the difference between two squares.  What are those numbers?  In other words, find $a$ and $b$ such that
$$a^2 - b^2 = 5,915,587,277$$

\textbf{Hint}: 5,915,587,277 is a prime number.

\section*{Solution}
$a^2 - b^2 = (a + b)(a - b).$  Given that the result is prime, it follows that either $a+b$ or $a-b$ must be 1 and the other quantity must equal the product (otherwise their product is not prime).  The only possibility that works is $a - b = 1$ and $a + b = 5,915,587,277$  so $a = 2,957,793,639$ and $b =  2,957,793,638.$
